    Sekhon

    Software Developer

    I enjoy making dynamic, original applications from beginning to end. Centered on creating intuitive experiences that adapt and advance in response to user measurements. I am a constant and quick learner. I enjoy collaborating with others to solve problems.

    My time at Damon Motorcycles began with a rush of excitement and nervous anticipation. Stepping into the fast-paced startup environment, I quickly learned that multitasking was not just a buzzword; it was a way of life. Each day presented new tasks, and I had to stay on my toes to keep up with the evolving demands of the projects I was involved in.

    Working in cross-functional teams became second nature to me, as we collaborated closely to bring innovative motorbike designs to life. The thrill of seeing our ideas take shape, from the drawing board to the workshop floor, was unlike anything I had experienced before. I knew then that I had found my calling in the world of programming and engineering.

    One aspect that stood out during my time at Damon was the importance of conducting thorough functionality tests. We knew that our motorcycles had to be not only cutting-edge in design but also reliable and safe. Endless hours were spent running simulations, analyzing data, and pushing the prototypes to their limits. Failure was part of the process, but each setback taught us valuable lessons and propelled us forward.

    As the responsibilities grew, I found myself taking on a unique role within the team. I was tasked with conducting in-depth research on critical modules that would define the overall direction of our motorbikes. Scaling was a top priority, and my investigations aimed to ensure that our designs could be adapted for various markets and customer needs.

    However, my work extended beyond coding and research. I discovered the art of bridging gaps among teams when project needs were ambiguous. This meant improving my communication skills, actively listening to others, and fostering an environment of collaboration and understanding. As I played the role of a mediator between different departments, I learned to approach challenges with empathy and diplomacy.

    My experiences at Damon Motorcycles not only made me a better developer but also a better teammate. Being entrusted with decision-making responsibilities allowed me to establish trust with my colleagues. I discovered that leadership was not just about making decisions but also about inspiring and guiding others to achieve their best potential.

    As the final chapter of my journey at Damon unfolded, I felt a sense of accomplishment and gratitude. The connections I had forged with my colleagues went beyond professional camaraderie; they were genuine friendships. Our collective efforts had resulted in the creation of remarkable motorbikes that were making a positive impact in the world.

    Leaving Damon Motorcycles wasn't easy, but I knew that the experiences and lessons I had gained there would stay with me forever. I moved on to new opportunities, carrying the passion and dedication instilled in me during my time at the startup. Whether it was tackling complex coding challenges or building strong teams, the story of Damon Motorcycles would forever be etched in the fabric of my personal and professional growth.
